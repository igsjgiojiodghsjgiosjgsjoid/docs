## Creating Your First Video

<Steps>
  <Step title="Sign Up">
    Sign up at [auraforcreators.com](https://auraforcreators.com) with your gmail
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose Video Mode">
    Select either "Aura Video" or "Seedance" from the model selector
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add Your Input">
    Upload a reference image (Aura Video) or write a detailed prompt (Seedance)
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select Quality">
    Choose 720p (4 credits) or 1080p (6 credits) - both are 5 seconds long
  </Step>
  <Step title="Generate & Download">
    Wait 60-120 seconds for your video to generate, then download
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Video Models Overview

### Aura Video (Image-to-Video)
- **Perfect for**: Animating existing images with realistic motion
- **Input**: Reference image + motion description
- **Best for**: Character animations, portrait videos, bringing photos to life

### Seedance (Text/Image-to-Video)  
- **Perfect for**: Creating hyper-realistic videos from scratch
- **Input**: Text prompt (with optional reference image)
- **Best for**: Cinematic scenes, complex motions, full video creation

## Creating Videos with Aura Video

<Steps>
  <Step title="Upload Reference Image">
    Upload a high-quality image as your starting frame
  </Step>

    Write a prompt describing the desired motion or action
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select Quality">
    Choose 720p (4 credits) or 1080p (6 credits)
  </Step>
  <Step title="Generate">
    Wait 60-90 seconds for generation to complete
  </Step>
  <Step title="Download">
    Download your MP4 video file
  </Step>
</Steps>

<CodeGroup>


### Advanced Prompt Structure

```text
[Subject] + [Action] + [Setting] + [Camera Movement] + [Style]

Example: "A confident entrepreneur walking through a modern office, camera tracking alongside, cinematic lighting, professional corporate environment"
```


## Common Issues & Solutions

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Jerky or unnatural motion">
    - Use gentler motion descriptions
    - Avoid complex multi-part actions
    - Focus on single, clear movements
    - Check reference image quality
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Motion doesn't match prompt">
    - Be more specific about the type of movement
    - Use motion-related keywords consistently
    - Avoid conflicting motion descriptions
    - Simplify complex action sequences
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Low quality results">
    - Ensure reference images are high resolution
    - Use descriptive, detailed prompts
    - Avoid heavily compressed source images
    - Consider using High quality setting
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
